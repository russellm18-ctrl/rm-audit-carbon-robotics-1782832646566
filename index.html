// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your LaserWeeder G2 and Carbon ATK product images are functioning as decorative files—no JSON-LD schema ties them to structured product markup, and there is zero evidence of descriptive alt-text connecting machine operation to commercial crop contexts like onions, lettuce, or carrots. A grower in Salinas Valley or Yuma right now searching "laser weeding machine for onions California" is landing on a competitor's page because your field imagery carries no embedded geographic, crop-specific, or equipment metadata. That is a direct, named, high-intent query you are invisible to despite owning the only commercial LaserWeeder on the market.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your large-scale commercial farm buyers—operations managers and directors of farming operations at the scale of Grimmway Farms or Duncan Family Farms—need visual confirmation of fleet-scale deployment before committing to a six-figure capital equipment decision. You have powerful written testimonials from named executives but zero field photography showing multiple LaserWeeder units operating simultaneously across commercial acreage. A single wide-format operational image showing row-scale laser precision on a real crop at real commercial density would close the credibility gap that your text cannot.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Precision ag competitors and autonomous equipment brands targeting the same Deere 6R and 8R operator base are publishing side-by-side machine comparison visuals, annotated field-result photography, and documented before/after crop imagery with timestamped yield callouts. You cite a validated 5–50% yield increase and a $700,000-to-$300,000 labor reduction from Hungenberg Produce, but neither number is anchored to a single photograph, field map, or visual data overlay—making your competitors' inferior claims look more credible because they are visually documented. That evidentiary gap is costing you mid-funnel conversions from exactly the decision-makers who vet equipment with procurement committees.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured operational shoot across at least three active customer sites—specifically crops like onions and leafy greens where your grower testimonials are already named—capturing machine-in-field, laser-on-weed close-up, cab interface, and side-by-side treated/untreated row sequences. Every image file leaves that shoot with embedded EXIF location data, crop type, machine model, and a consistent alt-text schema formatted as `[machine] [action] [crop] [location]` deployed uniformly across your CMS, structured product schema, and press assets. This converts your existing testimonial data—Riviera Farms' 70% chemical reduction, Hungenberg's $400K labor savings—into visually corroborated, search-indexable proof that compounds with every new grower story you publish.
